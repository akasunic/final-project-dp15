Resources

This site was created using the following tools:

  • D3 and Javascript
  • Google App Engine
  • Python
  • Jython Environment for Music
  • Bootstrap
  • Google Takeout

While creating our visualizations, various other email visualization pages/tools were referred to:

  • MIT Immersion
  • Stephen Wolfram's blog
  • Lots of ideas from Fernanda Viegas
  • Communication of Nicholas Felton

We also built off and were inspired by research conducted at Carnegie Mellon University and elsewhere, including:

  • Odom, William, John Zimmerman, and Jodi Forlizzi. Placelessness, spacelessness, and formlessness: experiential qualities of virtual possessions. Proceedings of the 2014 conference on Designing interactive systems. ACM, 2014
  • Belk, R. W. (1988). Possessions and the Extended Self. Journal of Consumer Research, 15(2), 139–168.
  • Cosley, D., & Sosik, V. (2012). Experiences With Designing Tools for Everyday Reminiscing.Human–Computer Interaction (September 2014), 175–198. doi:10.1080/07370024.2012.656047
  • Preliminary, unpublished exploration of personal email data visualization in Excel by students in Niki Kittur's P&T class, 2015 at Carnegie Mellon, under the guidance of John Zimmerman (Judeth Oden Choi, Xu Wang, Eunki Chung, Anna Kasunic).
