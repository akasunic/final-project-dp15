See-mail

Visualizing digital relationships

Digital communication has become common in every kind of relationship, be it professional or personal. Being able to go through communication patterns in personal relationships can help in understanding various aspects of the relationship such as whether the conversation has been two sided or has it been just one sided, whether the length of the communication has been long in times of distress or whether it has always maintained consistency etc. In this project we aim to visualize various kinds of friendships and to understand how the friendship has changed over time. We intend to understand if communication has been consistant over time, what topics were discussed, whether the communication was two sided or just one sided. To understasnd these aspects, we extracted emails which reflected the following types of friendships:

  • The Globetrotting Galpals

    We looked through emails exchanged between two friends who like to travel. We scraped through the email content to know the places they spoke about and visited. We plotted the various locations visited on a world map. Since the places were visited during different years, we plotted how many places were spoken about during each years using a histogram. Using a pie chart we plot how frequently each visited country was refered to.

  • The Flatmate Friends

    We looked at the emails exchanged between two flatmates to understand the balance in the friendship,i.e. whether one person was communicating more or were they both communicating equally. We also wanted to see how the communication changed between when the two were flatemates and when they were not. Using a timeline we plotted the communication history. Also we created a song for the friendship based on the communications, where higher notes represented person A, lower notes represented person B, the pitch was determined by the length of the email and the tempo was decided by the communication frequency.

  • The Buddy Bunch

    We looked at how email communication between a group of four friends varied over the years. We created two different forms of visualization to understand yearwise emailing frequency and month wise emailing frequency. To understand yearwise email communication, we created a treemap, while a histogram represented the monthwise frequency. To recap the content discussed in monthwise email exchange, we extracted short snippets from the emails which the user could see and could go back to the actual emails if something interesting came up. We also provide a keyword based search to remember when a particular topic was discussed.
